# Voice & Tone

## The voice in one sentence

A coach who runs with you on Sundays — knows your numbers, respects your time, doesn't over-explain.

## Principles

### 1. Direct, not curt
- **Yes**: "Skip the tempo if your achilles is still tight."
- **No**: "Listen to your body and make sure you take care of yourself out there!"

### 2. Specific over generic
Always cite the user's actual number when one exists.
- **Yes**: "Your last 4 weeks are +18 % vs the prior block — hold this load."
- **No**: "You've been training hard lately, keep it up!"

### 3. Active voice, second person
- **Yes**: "Add a 20-minute recovery walk on Friday."
- **No**: "A 20-minute recovery walk could be added on Friday."

### 4. No hype, no exclamation marks
We don't sell motivation. The user already has it — they showed up.
- **Banned words**: amazing, crush, smash, beast mode, let's go, you got this.
- **Allowed**: solid, on track, healthy, manageable, sustainable.

### 5. Calm under failure
When something breaks (API down, missed plan, bad input), the message is *what happened* and *what to do*, with no apology theatre.
- **Yes**: "Strava didn't return your activities. Refresh in a minute, or check your connection."
- **No**: "Oh no! Something went terribly wrong! We're so sorry! 😢"

### 6. French uses "tu"
The brand voice is informal in both languages. "Vous" reads as a marketing brochure. "Tu" reads as your training partner.

## Tone matrix

The voice stays constant. The tone shifts a little based on context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Onboarding | **Inviting** but matter-of-fact | "Connect your Strava account. We'll read the last 12 weeks." |
| Coaching feedback | **Confident, clinical** | "Volume spike — back off this week. +32 % vs prior block raises injury risk." |
| Training plan | **Instructive** | "5×1 km @ 4:35/km, 2 min jog. Cool down 10 min." |
| Chat coach | **Conversational, brief** | "Sleep matters more than the tempo run. Skip Tuesday, sleep, do Wednesday instead." |
| Errors | **Plain, calm** | "Goal date must be in the future." |
| Empty states | **Suggestive** | "No recent activity. Sync a run in Strava to unlock coaching." |

## Words we use

- **Athlete**, not "user".
- **Session** or **workout**, not "exercise".
- **Easy / threshold / tempo / VO2**, not "level 1 / 2 / 3".
- **Pace** (running), **power** (cycling), **CSS** (swimming).
- **Block** (a 3–4 week training period), **week**, **session**.

## Words we don't use

- "Crush", "smash", "destroy"
- "Beast", "warrior", "champion"
- "Just" ("just 30 minutes") — diminishes
- "Easy peasy", "no sweat"
- "Personalized journey", "your fitness adventure"

## Length

- **Coaching tips**: 1–3 sentences. If you need more, you're explaining instead of coaching.
- **Workout descriptions**: ≤ 4 sentences. The athlete is reading on their phone before heading out.
- **Chat replies (Gemini-generated)**: prompts already cap this. Don't loosen it.

## The "phone test"

Read every piece of UI copy out loud as if you were saying it to a friend at the trailhead. If it sounds like a brochure, a corporate Slack post, or a wellness influencer, rewrite it.
